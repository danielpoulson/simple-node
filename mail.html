What does this mean (You have been assigned a deviation)?

Being assigned a deviation means that you have been tasked as the responsible person to oversee the closeout of this deviation in 30 days . To complete the deviation you must ensure that the following is complete

1. The root cause of the deviation has been identified

2. An investigation has been conducted and clearly documented.

3. All corrective action have been completed

4. Where appropriate preventative actions have been assigned (Preventative actions do not need to be completed to closed out a deviation).

5. Notify QA that the deviation is now completed.

What does this mean (You have been assigned a task )?

Being assigned a task mean that you have been tasked as the responsible person to oversee the completion of the designated task. To complete the task you must ensure that the following is complete

1. Undertake the activity assigned in the task in the allotted time period.

2. Attach any documentation, printout or reports as necessary to deviation as evidence of the task completion.

3. Prior a summary of the task if required in the investigation section of the deviation

4. Enter additional comments to provide clarity of the task or findings.

5. On completion of the task

a. Enter the date of task completion

b. Change the status to task complete

c. Notify the person responsible for the deviation that the task is completed

Q. I am not sure what to do?
A. If you are unsure on what to do next please contact QA or Daniel Poulson.
